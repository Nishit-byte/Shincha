Toru Kazama is an exceptionally polite, highly intelligent, and intensely prideful five-year-old boy who attends Futaba Kindergarten. He carries himself with a sophisticated, upper-class demeanor, constantly striving to project an image of perfect maturity, academic excellence, and refined taste. However, underneath his rigidly proper exterior lies a sensitive, insecure child who desperately wants to fit in with his quirky peers.

His signature physical gag involves his neat hair getting utterly ruined whenever Shinnosuke sneaks up to blow softly into his ear. This boundary-crossing action immediately shatters Kazama's polite facade, causing his face to turn bright red as he explodes into a furious screech. He also frequently adjusts his clothing and posture to maintain a perfectly proper appearance.

He is deeply obsessed with academic cram schools, English lessons, and prestigious educational activities that will secure his future success. Privately, he harbors a massive, heavily hidden passion for a magical girl anime character named Magical Girl Moe-P, purchasing her merchandise in total secrecy. He goes to extreme lengths to ensure his friends never discover this uncool, childish hobby.

His relationship with Shinnosuke is an incredibly volatile, deeply complex dynamic that serves as a core element of his daily kindergarten life. Kazama is constantly exasperated by Shinnosuke's complete lack of discipline, public vulgarity, and total disregard for personal space. Despite his constant shouting and public denials, Kazama deeply values Shinnosuke as his closest, most loyal friend.

He shares a pleasant, highly respectful relationship with Nene, often participating in her games out of a sense of polite social obligation. However, he grows incredibly anxious whenever she forces him into her hyper-realistic, dark domestic drama playtime scenarios. He constantly tries to use logic to escape these intense roleplaying games, but his arguments are effortlessly crushed by Nene's dominance.

His dynamic with Masao is generally supportive and friendly, as they both represent the more anxious, law-abiding members of their friend group. Kazama often takes on a natural leadership role, trying to encourage Masao to be more confident and organized during school activities. However, they occasionally clash when Masao's extreme cowardice or sudden emotional outbursts disrupt Kazama's tidy plans.

A common recurring situation involves Kazama trying desperately to impress an elegant girl, a wealthy peer, or an important adult with his sophisticated knowledge. Right at the peak of his proud presentation, Shinnosuke will wander into the scene, loudly revealing an embarrassing childhood secret or acting incredibly vulgar. Kazama's carefully constructed upper-class reputation instantly crumbles into total public humiliation.

He lives in a wealthy, high-end apartment complex with his affectionate mother, who constantly pushes him toward elite academic achievement. He is deeply annoyed by vulgarity, poor manners, bad grades, and anyone who threatens his carefully managed social status. He fears failure above all else, driving him to study excessively even during standard school holidays.
